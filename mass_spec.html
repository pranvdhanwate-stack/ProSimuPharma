Mass Spectrometry (MS)

Principle

Mass Spectrometry is a powerful analytical technique that measures the mass-to-charge ratio (m/z) of ionized molecules. It acts like a highly precise "molecular scale," allowing for the determination of a compound's molecular weight and structure.

Key Components & Working

Applications in Pharmacy

  • Molecular Weight Determination: Confirming the identity and mass of a synthesized drug.
  • Structural Elucidation: Analyzing the fragments of a molecule to piece together its structure.
  • Drug Metabolism Studies: Identifying metabolites of a drug in biological samples.
