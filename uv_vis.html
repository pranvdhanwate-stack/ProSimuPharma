UV-Visible Spectroscopy

Principle

UV-Visible spectroscopy measures the absorption of UV or visible light by a substance. The amount of light absorbed is directly proportional to the concentration of the substance, a relationship described by the Beer-Lambert Law, making it ideal for quantitative analysis.

Key Components & Working

Applications in Pharmacy

  • Quantitative Analysis: To determine the precise concentration of an API in a solution.
  • Purity Determination: Can be used to detect impurities that absorb at different wavelengths.
  • Dissolution Testing: Used to measure the amount of drug dissolved over time.
