Gas Chromatography (GC)

Principle

Gas Chromatography is a separation technique used for analyzing volatile compounds. A sample is vaporized and injected into a carrier gas stream (the mobile phase), which carries it through a long, thin column containing a stationary phase. Components separate based on their boiling points and affinity for the stationary phase.

Key Components & Working

Applications in Pharmacy

  • Residual Solvent Analysis: Detecting trace amounts of organic solvents left over from the manufacturing process.
  • Purity of Volatile Raw Materials: Assessing the purity of starting materials like ethanol.
  • Analysis of Essential Oils: Separating and identifying the various components in natural product extracts.
