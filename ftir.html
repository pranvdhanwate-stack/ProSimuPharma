Fourier-Transform Infrared Spectroscopy (FTIR)

Principle

FTIR is an analytical technique used to identify organic and some inorganic materials. It works by passing infrared radiation through a sample. Different chemical bonds in a molecule absorb IR radiation at specific frequencies (or wavenumbers). The resulting pattern of absorption vs. wavenumber is a unique "fingerprint" of the molecule.

Key Components & Working

Applications in Pharmacy

  • Raw Material Identification: Quickly verifying the identity of incoming chemical ingredients.
  • Polymorph Analysis: Differentiating between different crystalline forms of a drug.
  • Functional Group Analysis: Identifying key chemical bonds in a newly synthesized molecule.
