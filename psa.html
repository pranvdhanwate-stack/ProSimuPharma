Advanced Particle Size Analyzer (PSA)

Principle

Particle Size Analysis using Dynamic Light Scattering (DLS) is a technique used to determine the size distribution of small particles in a suspension. It works by illuminating the particles with a laser and measuring the intensity fluctuations of the scattered light. These fluctuations are caused by the Brownian motion of the particlesâ€”smaller particles move faster, causing rapid fluctuations, while larger particles move slower, causing slower fluctuations. A mathematical analysis of these fluctuations provides the hydrodynamic diameter (size) and the polydispersity (size uniformity) of the particle population.

Applications in Pharmacy

  • Nanomedicine Development: Characterizing the size of nanoparticles, liposomes, and drug delivery systems.
  • Formulation Stability: Monitoring for particle aggregation or changes in size over time in suspensions and emulsions.
  • Quality Control: Ensuring batch-to-batch consistency of injectable products and sterile preparations.
