High-Performance Liquid Chromatography (HPLC)

Principle

HPLC operates on the principle of differential partitioning. A sample mixture is pumped in a liquid mobile phase at high pressure through a column packed with a solid stationary phase. Each component in the sample interacts differently with the stationary phase, causing them to travel through the column at different speeds and thus separate.

Key Components & Working

Applications in Pharmacy

  • Drug Purity and Assay: Quantifying the amount of active pharmaceutical ingredient (API).
  • Impurity Profiling: Identifying and quantifying unknown impurities in drug substances.
  • Stability Studies: Monitoring drug degradation over time under various conditions.
