Polarimetry

Principle

Polarimetry is a technique used to measure the angle of rotation caused by passing polarized light through an optically active (chiral) substance. Chiral molecules, which exist as non-superimposable mirror images (enantiomers), have the unique property of rotating the plane of polarized light. One enantiomer will rotate the light to the left (levorotatory, -), while its mirror image will rotate it to the right (dextrorotatory, +) by an equal and opposite amount.

Key Components & Working

Applications in Pharmacy

  • Quality Control: To confirm the identity and purity of chiral APIs, as often only one enantiomer is active.
  • Stability Studies: To detect if a pure enantiomer is degrading into a mixture (racemization).
  • Concentration Measurement: For known substances, the angle of rotation is proportional to concentration.
